Meeting at the boundaries of our worlds: [Skagafjörður](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Skagafj%C3%B6r%C3%B0ur), Iceland.

We want to strengthen, inspire, and engage the young members of the Polar community.

The total solar eclipse gives space to learn, think, and reflect. This is a philosophical, artistic, outdoor, and science workshop. Participants are expected to connect with nature and, through nature, with each other. The workshop aims to strengthen relationships among the young (“early-career”) polar community across borders. When datasets, funding, jobs, and subsequently a future are disappearing for a large part of the Polar community, it is time to strengthen our bonds. We envision this workshop as a step towards building stronger interpersonal connections and collaborations in the polar community - so we can continue to understand, capture, and protect the cryosphere in the future.

We encourage especially people with non-traditional careers/education to apply, as well as individuals who are underrepresented in the Polar community.

We commit to EDI principles. Names of participants will not be disclosed without their explicit consent.
